> [!abstract] Talk about managing a budget.
> - Why is it important to stick to a budget?
> - Who should have a budget?  
> - Do you personally have a budget?  

Managing a budget is important because it helps you <u>control your spending, save for future needs, and avoid unnecessary debt</u>.   
By tracking your income and expenses, you can make sure you’re <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">living within your means and planning for both short-term and long-term goals</mark>. Everyone should have a budget—students, working adults, and even families. For students, a budget helps manage school expenses and daily costs. For adults, it can support saving for a house, a car, or retirement. I personally have a simple budget that I update each month. I list my income, like part-time job earnings, and then plan how much to spend on food, transport, school supplies, and savings. It helps me avoid spending too much on non-essentials and reminds me to save for important things. Having a budget gives me peace of mind and helps me feel more in control of my finances. It’s a useful habit that benefits anyone.
