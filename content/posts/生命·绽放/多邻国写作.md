> [!question]
> Do you believe that watching a movie adaptation of a book is just as good as reading the original? Explain your opinion with supporting reasons.  

I do not agree that watching a movie adaptation of a book is just as good as reading the original. Reading the original book allows readers to <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">grasp</mark> what the author wants to express initially. If one only watches the adaptation for convenience, they may miss the author’s <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">original intentions</mark> and even <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">lose some essential details</mark> in the book.

For instance, take China’s famous novel _Journey to the West_ as an example. Many people watch its movie adaptations and think they already understand what the novel is trying to <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">convey</mark>. In reality, they only know the <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">basic scenes</mark> and characters, but <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">overlook</mark> the core of the story—such as the <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">spiritual pursuit of perseverance</mark> and <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">enlightenment</mark> that the author aimed to inspire in readers.

Moreover, movies are often limited by runtime, so they have to simplify complex plots or omit subtle psychological descriptions of characters. In contrast, the original book provides rich inner <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">monologues</mark> 独白 and <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">contextual details</mark>, helping readers fully connect with the author’s thoughts.

Therefore, it is a better choice to read the original work if one wants to truly understand a book.
