## 雅思口语高级词汇  

laborious = difficult (job, task)  
laborious job / task  

culmination = the end (series of events)  

### exacerbate 使加剧  
exacerbate = to make something worse  
exacerbate a problem / situation / impact / effect 
The problem of climate change is exacerbating by people flying too much and creating carbon emissions.  

### mitigate 减轻，缓和  
mitigate = make less severe, less unpleasant  
mitigate a problem / impact /effect / suffering  
We could mitigate the impact of climate change by flying less  

### insurmountable adj. 无法克服的, 难以解决的  
insurmountable = impossibe to solve  
insurmountable problem / challenge / barrier  
Although climate change is a challenging problem, it's not insurmountable, we can solve it.  

### an uphill struggle 一件非常艰难、需要付出极大努力才能完成的事情  
an uphill struggle = difficult / challenging but possible  
Writing my new book has been an uphill struggle, so I will be thrilled when it's finished.  

### apprehensive 忧虑的  
apprehensive = worried, nervous (about a bad result)  
apprehensive about  
Initially, I was really apprehensive about doing my driving test, but in the end, it turned out really well.  

### disgruntled 不满的  
disgruntled = unhappy and disappointed / dissatisfied  
The customer service in the shop was terrible and I felt rather disgruntled.  

### bewildered 糊涂的  
bewildered = confused  
thoroughly / utterly bewildered by
I was utterly bewildered by my friend's decision to quit his high paid job.  

to do my upmost = to try my best  
The government have produced a good economic plan, and I am sure they will do their upmost to implement it.  

### inept adj.无能的  
inept = not good at your job, showing a lack of skill  
vary / rather / totally / politically / socially inept at  
My boss like to design his own PowerPoints, but between you and me, he is rather inept at it.  

### impeccable 无错误的  
impeccable = perfect, without fault
an impeccable job / behaviour / manners / English
My friend has lived in America and she speaks impeccable English.

### 一、与工作 / 学习相关

- **To knock off**：有多种含义，在工作相关语境中表示 “结束工作”，例如 “I knock off at 7pm” 即 “我晚上 7 点下班”。发音上，当前后单词分别以辅音和元音开头时可连读，如 “knock off” 可读作 /nɒkɒf/，过去式 “knocked off” 读作 /nɒktɒf/。
- **To chip in**：意为 “打断并发表评论”，可用于会议等场景，比如 “Excuse me, can I just chip in?”（打扰一下，我能插句话吗？），也可表示他人在会议中的贡献，如 “He chipped in with some good ideas”（他插话提出了一些好主意）。
- **To hash out something**：指 “与某人讨论某事以达成协议”，例如 “We need to hash out the details of our presentation”（我们需要讨论出演示文稿的细节）。

### 二、与人物相关

- **To wind someone up**：表示 “惹恼某人”，如 “She really winds me up when she says teachers have an easy life”（当她说老师生活轻松时，真的让我很恼火）。
- **To make someone out**：意为 “看清、听清或理解某人 / 某事”，比如 “I can’t make out your handwriting”（我看不懂你的笔迹），“I can’t make you out”（我不理解你的行为）。

### 三、与地点相关

- **To do up**：表示 “装修（房间、房子、公寓等）”，例如 “I have just done up the bathroom and it looks as good as new”（我刚装修完浴室，它看起来像新的一样）。
- **To drop in (on)**：意为 “拜访某人 / 某地，通常是未经事先邀请的”，如 “I am visiting New Delhi next week, I might drop in on you”（我下周要去新德里，可能会顺便拜访你）。类似的表达还有 “I went round to Jacob’s (house)”“I popped round to Jacob’s (U.K.)” 等。

### 四、与事件相关

- **To turn out**：表示 “以某种方式发生”，例如 “My presentation turned out badly, I forgot everything”（我的演示搞砸了，我什么都忘了）。
- **To go off**：指 “以特定方式进行（通常是顺利的）”，如 “The meeting went off well”（会议进行得很顺利）。

### 五、与活动相关

- **To take up (a hobby)**：意为 “开始（一项爱好）”；**To take to (a hobby)**：表示 “非常喜欢并擅长（一项爱好）”，例如 “My daughter started aikido recently and she took to it really well”（我女儿最近开始学合气道，而且学得很好）。
- **To show off**：指 “做某事试图向别人展示自己有多棒（令人讨厌地）”，如 “At school, the teacher’s pet was always showing off”（在学校，老师的宠儿总是爱炫耀）。
- **To rub someone up the wrong way**：表示 “惹恼某人（并非故意）”，例如 “When the teacher’s pet shows off, it rubs me up the wrong way”（当老师的宠儿炫耀时，让我很不舒服）。

### 六、额外短语

- **To nod off**：意为 “睡着”，如 “The meeting was so boring, I nodded off!”（会议太无聊了，我睡着了！）。
